Ethereum Sea

Ethereum

No one really knows what the ethereum is. People know more about what it is not than what it is. It is an odorless substance that has some of the properties of a gas and a liquid. It is dense enough that it can make hundred mile wide islands of earth and stone float like they were icebergs. But at the same time people are able to easily pierce its depths by swimming through it and they can breathe it as easily as they can breathe air.

Anything that “falls” into the ethereum simply floats there at island level as if they were in water. However, it is far more forgiving that water. Even falling from a great height - objects (people) simply plummet into the deep ethereum until popping up like a cork in water. Also, no one drowns in ethereum, since it can be breathed like air. The biggest problems for those afloat are thirst, starvation, and the predators that make their home in the sea.

The ethereum is clear down to a hundred feet or so and then it begins to cloud over. Those that dare the deep say the deeper you go is like an ever encroaching mist until you cannot see beyond your reach. But few dare to dive too deep because many simply have not come back.

Common, shallow, ethereum cannot be contained. You can push a jar down in it, put an air tight top on it, and still come up with nothing. However, down deep and around islands funny things can happen to ethereum. It sometimes congeals into a real liquid that can be contained. And even less often solid ethereum can be found in the deep. These special forms of the substance are called ether. The Klik have been using this ether to power their unique mechano-wizardry gadgets for generations. But it is only recently that others have found ways to use it to power and propel the great ethereumships and aircraft of the day.

Day and Night

There is no sun or moon in the sky over the Ethereum Sea. At first light the sky lightens from the black of night to the soft golden yellow glow of full day. Since there is no sun and the light comes from all directions there are no shadows. The length of the day and night over the sea is not constant - it varies from region to region throughout the year, but the same schedule repeats every year.

The dayglow is constant throughout the day until evening when it fades over the course of an hour until the sky goes black. It is not completely dark however because countless stars dot the sky. The stars have a permanent position, but they each have a finite life - they do not last forever. Some stars have been in the sky for life times, others last only a couple years or even a few months. Nations and organization chart the night sky daily in order to be able to navigate effectively.
